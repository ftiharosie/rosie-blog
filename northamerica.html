NORTH AMERICA

CANCUN:



The gorgeous beaches of white sand caressed by warm, turquoise waters of the Caribbean Sea stand out as the hottest spots in town, both literally and figuratively. It's impossible not to enjoy them so don't even try. Put on your bikini or swimsuit, it's time for fun under the sun!

NIAGARA FALLS, CANADA:



Straddling the US-Canadian border, Niagara Falls brings around 12 million visitors per year.The Niagara Falls is actually composed of three falls: American, Bridal and Horseshoe. They come together over a 170-foot dolomite and shale cliff to plummet to the raging river below. All three waterfalls form the area that is collectively referred to as Niagara Falls, which reaches into the province of Ontario and New York state.The falls can produce 4 million kilowatts of electricity.

LOS ANGELES:



Los Angeles, is a great city, where there are many great beaches to visit. Other beaches are more vibrant than others. So, grab your beach bag and get to the beach to just sit back and relax!

PUERTO PLATA:



Puerto Plata is a beautiful scenic area with picturesque beaches with a breathtaking mountain backdrops.

SANTA MONICA:



Santa Monica is a beachfront city in western Los Angeles County, California, United States. Situated on Santa Monica Bay, it is bordered on three sides by the city of Los Angeles.There are many activities to do in Santa Monica such as: walking on the boardwalk, purchasing items,and riding the amusment rides!

PUNTA CANA:



Punta Cana is the name of a region in the easternmost tip of the Dominican Republic.Punta Cana has a tropical climate. Although it is mildly windy, the ocean in the area is mainly shallow, with several natural marine pools in which visitors can bathe. The weather is fairly constant, with an average temperature of 26°C.

LOS CABOS:



Part of the fun comes with exploring the region’s two dramatically distinct personas. Tranquil San Jose del Cabo retains the look and vibe of an authentic Mexican town. Cobblestone streets, intimate restaurants and boutiques radiate from the central main square and mission church. Rambunctious Cabo San Lucas, on the other end of the highway (called the Corridor), is party central with funky bars and the slick Luxury Avenue Mall centered around the marina.On the shores of the Sea of Cortes lies El Chileno, a quiet beach which is part of the Tourist Corridor of Los Cabos, next to Costa Azul, Punta Palmilla, el Bledito Bay, Santa María Bay and Cabo San Lucas Bay. Here you will find a wide range of hotels offering first class services, as well as dozens of cruise ships looking forward to the spectacular sunset.
